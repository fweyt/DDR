# General medical knowledge (reference)

## Fever in adults
- < 38°C: no fever
- 38–39°C: moderate fever, rest and paracetamol (max 4g/day)
- > 39°C: high fever, drink plenty, paracetamol, consult a doctor if > 3 days

## Dehydration
Signs: thirst, dry mouth, dark urine, little urination, dizziness.
Advice: ORS or water with salt/sugar, frequent small sips.

## Wounds
- Clean with water, disinfect with iodine or chlorhexidine
- Butterfly bandage or sterile dressing
- Check tetanus vaccination (every 10 years)
- Redness/warmth/pus → wound infection → GP

## Medication
- Paracetamol: 500–1000 mg per dose, max 4g/day (2g with liver problems)
- Ibuprofen: 400 mg per dose, max 1200 mg/day, not on empty stomach
- NO aspirin for children < 12 years (risk of Reye's syndrome)

## Disclaimer
These guidelines are indicative. They do not replace a medical consultation. When in doubt, always consult a doctor.
